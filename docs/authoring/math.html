Math

Calepin renders LaTeX math notation using KaTeX (HTML, optionally MathJax) or natively (LaTeX and Typst). Use \$ for a literal dollar sign.

Inline and display math

💡 Click </> here or in the navbar to view this page's source side-by-side.

Inline math is enclosed in single dollar signs: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Display math uses double dollar signs:

(1) $$ \int_0^1 x^2 \, dx = \frac{1}{3} $$

Multi-line equations use \begin{aligned} inside double dollar signs:

$$ \begin{aligned} \nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0} \\ \nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} &= 0 \\ \nabla \times \mathbf{E} &= -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \\ \nabla \times \mathbf{B} &= \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t} \end{aligned} $$

And we can use standard cross-references as in Equation 1.

Theorem environments

Fenced divs with theorem-type classes get automatic numbering and can be cross-referenced.

Theorem 1.

In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Proof.

Let a right triangle have legs $a$, $b$ and hypotenuse $c$. By constructing squares on each side and comparing areas, we obtain $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

□

By Theorem 1, the relationship is fundamental to Euclidean geometry.

Supported types

The following theorem types are available, each with its own counter:

theorem, lemma, corollary, proposition, conjecture, definition, example, exercise, solution, remark, and algorithm.

Cross-references

Fenced divs with reserved id prefixes participate in the cross-reference system:

Class Prefix Example
theorem thm ::: {.theorem #thm-pythagoras}
lemma lem ::: {.lemma #lem-helper}
definition def ::: {.definition #def-group}
example exm ::: {.example #exm-basic}

References like @thm-pythagoras resolve to the theorem number. The {{label}} variable generates format-specific anchors: \label{thm-pythagoras} (LaTeX), <thm-pythagoras> (Typst).
